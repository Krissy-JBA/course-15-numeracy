Sales targets

A sales target is a goal that gets set for a salesperson or sales department and is measured in revenue or units sold over a specific period of time.

Costs

In business and accounting, when we talk about cost, we’re talking about the monetary value that a company spends in order to produce something. So, cost is the amount of money a company spends on creating or producing goods or services.

Profit growth

Profit is how much money a company makes after deducting expenses, costs and taxes - and profit growth is how much that money increases. Companies work out their profit growth by calculating the percentage the profit has increased from one period to another (so when you see profit growth written down, it’ll be expressed as a percentage).

Productivity

Productivity is an average measure of how efficient production is. It can be expressed as the ratio of output to inputs used in the production process, i.e. how much output you get per unit of input! When all outputs and inputs are included in the productivity measure, it’s called ‘total productivity’.

Gross margin

Gross margin is the difference between revenue and the Cost Of Goods Sold (often called COGS), divided by revenue. You’ll see it written and expressed as a percentage. It’s usually calculated as the selling price of an item, minus the cost of the prodcution, creation or acquistion of the goods that have been sold.

Monthly profit

Monthly profit is the amount of money a company makes in a month – after deducting expenses, costs and taxes.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC – bit of an unfortunate nickname!) is the cost associated with convincing a customer to buy a product or service. This is what it actually costs an organisation to persuade a potential customer. It includes the product cost as well as the cost involved in research, marketing, and accessibility.

ARPU

Average Revenue Per User (also known as average revenue per unit), usually gets abbreviated to ARPU. It’s a measure mainly used by consumer communications and networking companies, and is typically defined as the total revenue divided by the number of subscribers.

Operating performance

This is a measure of how well a company’s doing. When you’re evaluating operating performance you need to take a number of different measures into account, like expense ratios, sales performance and policy terminations. This is numeracy in the business world.
