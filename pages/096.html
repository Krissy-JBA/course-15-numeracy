Customer acquisition cost

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is the cost associated with convincing a customer to buy a product or service. This is what it actually costs an organisation to persuade a potential customer! It includes the product cost as well as the cost involved in research, marketing, and accessibility. It’s caluclated using the following formula:

Sales, marketing and advertising costs for the period ÷ number of customers gained over that same period.

To reduce CAC

  • Reduce advertising that doesn’t perform well
  • Spend more in high performing areas

When deciding between different marketing campaigns, you should always consider CAC!
