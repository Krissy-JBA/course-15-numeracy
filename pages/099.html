Lifetime value of a customer

To calculate the lifetime value of a customer, we need to know the average length of time a business retains a customer (usually expressed in years). Zoey’s business keeps customers for 7 years. Let’s picture her business sometime in the future and imagine it being really successful!

We’ll say that:

Average revenue per year is $2,000

Average costs per year are $1,500

So, lifetime value = No of years a customer will be retained x (average revenue per year – average costs per year). Have a go below!
