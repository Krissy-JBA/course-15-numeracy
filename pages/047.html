Imperial and metric

The older imperial units like feet, pints, ounces and miles are still used alongside metric. The metric system is based on the metre, litre, and gram as units of length, capacity, weight or mass. Most countries use the metric system, this is the one that adds prefixes like kilo-, milli- and centi- to count orders of magnitude.

Length and distance

A centimetre is about the width of your finger. A metre is about as long as a stride. Length and distance are basically the measurement between two points.

Capacity

This is a measure of how much something holds. For example, how many rolls of fabric can Zoey's shelves hold? Or how much water does a cup hold?

Weight

This is the heaviness of an object or thing. Think about the relationship between capacity and weight. Think about the fact that Zoey might be able to fit 3 sewing machines on a work bench, but that the work bench might only be able to handle the weight of two! Zoey needs to know how to make these decisions.

Time and dates

Time and date are non-decimal systems (so be careful with adding hours and minutes!) Zoey needs to be able to measure these and make decisions about workflows, meetings, employing staff and a whole heap of other things.

Money

Money’s based on the decimal system. So $0.01 would be 1 cent.
