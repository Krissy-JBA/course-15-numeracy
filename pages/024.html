Challenge thinking

In 2013, a study by the smart folk at the National Centre of Education and Economy found that “the mathematics that most enables students to be successful in college courses is not high school mathematics, but middles school mathematics, especially in arithmetic, ratio, proportion, expressions and simple equations.”

This is quite different from how people traditionally see maths abilities. After all, when it comes to maths, what’s more valuable – knowing how to do complex calculations – or being able to apply it accurately via numeracy skills in everyday life?
