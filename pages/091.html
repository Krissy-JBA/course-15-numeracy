Cash Burn

Cash burn is the rate at which a new business uses up its money to pay expenses before it becomes ‘cashflow positive’ (i.e. has more cash flowing in, than out!) It’s important while sales are just getting started, while new customers are being discovered, and while the business is growing. When starting up a business it’s common for costs to be bigger than income and that’s where cash burn comes in. It’s the rate at which the business spends its capital or savings, and is expressed as a period of time, usually per month or per week.

Cash burn is calculated using the following formula:

Operating cash spend for the period – operating cash income for the period = Cash burn for the period.
