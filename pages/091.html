Cash burn

Cash burn is the rate at which a new business uses up its money to pay expenses before it becomes ‘cashflow positive’ (i.e. has more cash flowing in, than out!) It’s really important while sales are just getting started, while new customers are being discovered, and while the business is growing. We’ll use an example to look at this in detail shortly.

Cashflow runway

Cashflow runway is the length of time a business can continue to operate before it uses up all of its cash reserves!

We’ve got a handy example of this coming up, so you can look at this in a bit more detail.

Inventory

Inventory’s made up of raw materials, work-in-process and finished goods that are ready for sale. For most businesses, inventory is one of the most important assets they possess. Converting inventory into sales is one of the main sources of revenue and profit for business owners. (It’s also one of the biggest assets most businesses will have!)

In Zoey's business, this would include.

  • Fixtures and fitting – shelves and desks
  • Sewing Machines
  • Fabric supplies
  • Tools – scissors, pins, cotton
  • Computer hardware
  • Clothing – garments made ready for sale
