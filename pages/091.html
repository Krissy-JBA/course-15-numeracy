Customer loyalty

Customer loyalty is about how likely previous customers are to use your business again and how much they favour your brand over others. Customer loyalty is a result of customers having a consistently good experience with your business – e.g. being satisfied with a product or service, enjoying the convenience, or simply being comfortable and familiar with a brand.

Customer retention

Customer retention refers to the activities that a business carries out in order to retain as many customers as possible (and reduce the number it loses!) Successful customer retention starts with the very first contact a business has with a customer and continues throughout the entire lifetime of that relationship.

Customer satisfaction

Customer satisfaction measures how satisfied customers are with your products or services (i.e. whether they meet or exceed your customers’ expectations). This one’s important because it provides marketers and business owners with a clear metric that they can use to manage and improve their businesses.

Net promoter score

Net promoter score is all about ‘word of mouth!’ This might well be the most popular way of measuring your clients' loyalty. It measures how likely customers are to refer you to someone else. The customer is asked how likely they would be to recommend a business, using a scale from 1 to 10.

Lifetime value

Lifetime value (also called customer lifetime value, or CLV) is a prediction of how much a customer is likely to be worth to a business (in terms of value or net profit) over the entire future relationship between business and customer.
